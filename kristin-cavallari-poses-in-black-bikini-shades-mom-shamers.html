    Kristin Cavallari is proudly flaunting her fit figure regardless of what the haters may say.

    The “Laguna Beach” alum — who shares sons Camden, 10, Jaxon, 8, and daughter Saylor, 7, with ex-husband Jay Cutler — posted a carousel of racy snaps of herself in a sexy black bikini in an effort to clap back at people who have criticized her wardrobe choices as a mother.

    In the images, Cavallari, 36, can be seen seductively sitting on a table as she shows off her toned physique.

    She also wore her hair up in a messy ponytail as she looked at the camera and posed.

    “…but she’s a mother 😱,” the “Very Cavallari” alum captioned the Instagram post.

    Cavallari was praised by many — including other moms — in the comments section.

    “Who says moms can’t be hot,” wrote one fan, while another added, “Thank you for being an inspiration, we can have more sides than just being moms ❤️🙌🔥.”

    “Well ya can’t tell she’s a mother by that bod so there’s that you ….you mo fo’s & actually in this forum? It’s all that matters . Haters. Love, a fellow hot mom. ❤️🔥🙌,” added a third supporter.

    “Love this! The amount of times mothers get crap for wanting to be themselves is awful. You look amazing and happy and that’s what matters.. spread the confidence girl. Love it 🔥👏,” commented a fourth person.

    While Cavallari did not disclose to whom her shady caption was directed, she has not been shy about celebrating her body in the past.

    Last summer, the “Uncommon James” founder announced she had gained some weight in the last few years after being “shocked at how thin” she looked in old photographs.

    “I didn’t realize it at the time, how thin I was,” she told Us Weekly in June 2022. “So, I’m happy that I’ve put on weight.”

    A few days later, Cavallari showed off her weight gain progress by posting a butt-baring bikini photo online.

    She captioned the Instagram post in part, “I work out really hard and am dedicated to a healthy lifestyle and I think just because I’m a mom doesn’t mean I can’t be proud of my body and be comfortable in my skin.”
