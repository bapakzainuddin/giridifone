    Who is Alain Ducasse?

    Alain Ducasse is a French-born Monégasque chef. Currently, he owns an enormous number of famous restaurants in The Dorchester, in London and has received three Michelin Guide stars.

    Furthermore, his other renowned hotels include Restaurant Louis XV, Restaurant Le Jules Verne, Restaurant Le Meurice, and much more.

    Back in 2013, he was awarded the Lifetime Achievement of The World’s 50 Best Restaurants List. Apart from that, the legendary chef has been a special guest in the US and Italian versions of MasterChef.

    Alain Ducasse: Early Life, Childhood, and Education

    Alain was born on 13 September 1956, in Orthez, France. Talking about his nationality, he is a French-born chef and holds Monaco citizenship.

    Furthermore, his ethnicity is unknown. From the beginning of his childhood, he had a keen interest in cooking. Concerning his education, he joined on a farm in Castel-Sarrazin.

    Later in 1972, he started an apprenticeship at the Pavillon Landais restaurant in Soustons and at the Bordeaux hotel school. Soon after completing his apprenticeship, Alain began work at Michel Guérard’s restaurant in Eugénie-Les-Bains.

    Alain Ducasse: Career, Net Worth, and Awards

    Alain began his professional chef career as an assistant at Moulin de Mougins under legendary chef Roger Vergé.

    In 1980, he became a chef at L’amandier in Mougins. Back in 1986, Alain was offered the Chef position at the Hôtel de Paris in Monte Carlo.

    After he performed really well, he continued to run management. Furthermore, he launched Alain Ducasse restaurant in Le Parc – Sofitel Demeure Hôtels in the 16th arrondissement of Paris. Just after eight months after opening, his restaurant The Red Guide awarded the restaurant three stars.

    Moreover, the professional chef opened Adour, at the St. Regis Hotel on 16th and K Street in Washington, D.C. in 2008. Soon after that, he also opened a more casual Bistro Benoit New York, at 60 West 55th Street.

    For Prince Albert, his bride Charlene Wittstock, he prepared a multi-course gala dinner on  2 July 2011. Additionally, he also became the first chef to own restaurants carrying three Michelin Stars in three cities.

    Besides this, he is the only one of two chefs to hold 21 Michelin stars throughout his career. Furthermore, his other renowned hotels include Restaurant Louis XV, Restaurant Le Jules Verne, Restaurant Le Meurice, and much more.

    Being a professional chef and owner of top restaurants around the world, Alain pockets a massive amount of money from his profession. Currently, he earns millions of dollars.
