    Students Hospitalized After Knife Incident at Monhagen Middle School

    A local Orange County middle school remains in lockdown after authorities responded to a disturbing knife incident that sent two students to the hospital earlier this morning.

    The harrowing event unfolded at Middletown’s Monhagen Middle School around breakfast time, according to witnesses. Officials say a confrontation between students escalated rapidly, resulting in both injuries and a massive law enforcement response.

    Disturbing Physical Altercation in Front of Peers

    Eighth grader Jaiden Mojica was among the crowd of students who shockingly witnessed the violent clash firsthand. He recalled seeing one victim walking while clutching their side afterwards.

    “I saw a student walking like this (holding side), I assume that’s the one that got stabbed,” described Mojica. “I came back from breakfast over to the eighth-grade wing where I saw a big crowd. The fight had just finished and then the kid was walking from there this way. So I assume he was going to the nurse.”

    While specifics remain under investigation, officials have indicated a “student-on-student” incident involving some type of stab or puncture wounds. Both injured pupils were quickly transported to receive emergency medical care.

    Entire Middle School Placed Under Lockdown

    In response to an undoubtedly terrifying situation for the school community, authorities immediately secured the entire Monhagen campus.

    As per strict lockdown protocols, all external building doors were secured against potential outside threats while police canvassed school grounds. Officials confirmed no intruder entered facilities, deeming it an isolated clash.

    Superintendent Amy Creeden acknowledged the traumatic impact this incident has inflicted on vulnerable young minds. “What happened today is not OK,” she affirmed. “It is very concerning, it is upsetting, My heart hurts.”

    Counseling resources stand at the ready to support families, students and staff struggling to process events.

    Investigation Ongoing as Suspects Undergo Police Custody

    Middletown law enforcement officials remain actively investigating circumstances leading up to the altercation. All involved parties now remain in police custody, though identities and charges have not yet been disclosed.

    Authorities say today’s priority is maintaining security and facilitating a safe early release for Monhagen students by 9:45am. Nearby Maple Hill Elementary enacted limited lockout protocols as an added precaution.

    Seeking Community Support and Healing

    As we anxiously await additional details, Superintendent Creeden implored that “our young people and our school community need us.”

    This harrowing incident has left deep wounds, both physical and emotional. Middletown must now come together, as families and concerned citizens, to nurture those reeling from this trauma.

    By providing robust support and guidance to our youth during this crisis moment, we can start rebuilding the trust and safety stolen today. Our children deserve no less.
