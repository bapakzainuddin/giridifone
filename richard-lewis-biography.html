    Who is Richard Lewis?

    Richard Lewis is an American comedian and actor.

    He is known for co-starring in the comedy series Anything but Love (1989-92) and for his recurring and semi-autobiographical role in Curb Your Enthusiasm (2000-present).

    Richard Lewis: Age, Parents, Ethnicity

    Richard was born Richard Philip Lewis in Brooklyn, New York, the United States on June 29, 1947, to parents Blanche Lewis. As of 2021, he is 74 years old.

    He belongs to American nationality and English ethnicity. His birth sign is Cancer.

    Education

    Talking about his education, firstly, he attended Dwight Morrow High School. Then, he attended Ohio State University.

    Richard Lewis: Professional life, Career

    Talking about his profession, his first professional comic stint was with New York’s Improvisation Comedy Club in the early 1970s and by 1974 he managed to appear on The Tonight.

    Likewise, he started making regular appearances on The Sonny and Cher Show. Similarly, he did many little stints for shows like Saturday Night Live, made numerous television appearances, and toured along with other comics on—especially the comic concert with Jamie Lee Curtis.

    Moreover, he acted with Don Rickles in the short-lived TV series ‘Daddy Dearest’. Similarly, he had a recurring role in ‘Rude Awakening’, as ‘Rabbi Richard Glass’ on the dramatic series ‘7th Heaven’, and appeared in the ‘Tales from the Crypt’. He was also endorsing popular brands in the 1990s, like, Boku, Snapple, and Certs breath mints.

    In addition, he is also an author and apart from writing comic articles and columns for various American magazines, he released his own book in 2001.

    Similarly, he is recent cameos were in ‘Everybody Hates Chris along with Chris Rock and as Charlie Sheen’s accountant in ‘Two and a Half Men’.

    Therefore, he has also completed filming “Squirrels To The Nuts”, a Peter Bogdanovich film due for release in 2014, starring Jennifer Aniston, Owen Wilson, Imogen Poots, and Cybill Shepherd, among others.

    Awards

    Talking about his lifetime achievements and awards, he won Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Comedy Series for Seinfeld (1989), he won EDA Special Mention Award for Most Egregious Age Difference Between the Leading Man and the Love Interest for Whatever Works (2009), he won Golden Nymph Award for Outstanding Actor – Comedy Series for Curb Your Enthusiasm (2000).
