    Who is Barbara Walters?

    Barbara Walters is an American broadcast journalist, author, and television personality. Similarly, she is well known as Barbara Walters and Barbara Jill Walters.

    Likewise, she is also known as a television personality in the early 1960s, when she was a writer and segment producer of “women’s interest stories”.

    Who are the parents of Barbara Walters?

    Barbara was born in Boston, Massachusetts, the United States on September 25, 1929, to parents Louis Edward Walters (father) and Dena Seletsky (mother).

    Barbara Walters has siblings namely, Burton Walters and Jacqueline Walters. She belongs of American nationality and Ashkenazi Jewish ethnicity. Her birth sign is Libra.

    Talking about her education, firstly, Barbara Walters attended Miami Beach Senior High School. Then, she attended the Ethical Culture Fieldston School. Likewise, she attended the Birch Wathen Lenox School. Lastly, she attended Sarah Lawrence College.

    Barbara Walters: Early Professional Career

    Talking about Barbara Walters’s profession, in 1953, she produced a 15- minute children’s program, ‘Ask the Camera’ which was directed by Roone Arledge. In 1955 she also became a writer for ‘The Morning Show’ at CBS. She joined Tex McCrary Inc.

    Similarly, she joined NBC’s ‘The Today Show’ as a writer and researcher in 1961 and soon became the show’s regular ‘Today Girl’. Likewise, she began developing and writing her reports and interviews. Similarly, she also became the first female co-host of ‘The Today Show’ in 1974.

    Therefore, while working with ABC she had served as a commentator on news specials like presidential inaugurations and as a moderator between candidates Jimmy Carter and Gerald Ford during the 1976 Presidential Elections.

    Similarly, Barbara Walters joined the ABC newsmagazine ’20/20’ in 1979 and became a regular special contributor by 1981. Therefore, she interviewed leaders from all over the world including UK Margaret Thatcher, Cuba’s Fidel Castro, Russia’s Boris Yeltsin, and Venezuelan President, Hugo Chavez.

    Moreover, Barbara Walters has also interviewed celebrity icons like Michael Jackson, Sir Laurence Olivier and Katharine Hepburn. Besides that, she along with Bill Geddie conceived the idea for the talk show ‘The View’ that has aired on ABC since August 1997. Whereas, she took semi-retirement in 2004. She also continued to co-host ‘The View’.

    Barbara Walters: Awards, Nominations

    Taking about her lifetime achievements and awards, she won the Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Informational Series for The Barbara Walters Special (1976), and she won Daytime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Talk Show Host for The View (1997).

    Likewise, she won Daytime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Host or Hostess in a Talk, Service, or Variety Series for Today (1952).
